Pantry & Ingredient Guide

A reference for everything used across these recipes — how to store it, how long it lasts, shelf-stable or freezer alternatives, and which recipes need it.

🟢 Long-lasting / shelf-stable 🟡 Medium (weeks) 🔴 Short (days)
Fresh Produce

When a fresh ingredient has a shelf-stable or freezer alternative, the Notes column points to it.

Ingredient Storage Shelf Life Notes
Onion Cool, dark, dry pantry (NOT fridge) 1–3 months Always keep stocked. Cut onion goes in fridge, use within 1 week.
Green onion (scallion) Fridge 1–2 weeks Always keep stocked. Used as garnish across many recipes.
Shallots Cool, dark, dry pantry 1–2 months Always keep stocked. Same rules as onion.
Garlic Cool, dark, dry pantry 1–3 months (whole head) Buy as needed. → Canned & Jarred: Jarred Minced Garlic for a longer-lasting alternative (works for cooked dishes; noticeable drop in minimalist dishes like aglio e olio).
Ginger Fridge (unpeeled) 3–4 weeks → Freezer Staples: Ginger — freeze whole and grate directly from frozen, no peeling needed. Jarred minced ginger as backup.
Tomatoes Counter (NOT fridge) ~1 week → Canned & Jarred: Diced/Crushed Tomatoes — 1 can (400g) ≈ 3 fresh. Minimal flavor difference in cooked dishes. Canned recommended.
Bell peppers Fridge 1–2 weeks whole → Canned & Jarred: Jarred Roasted Red Peppers or → Freezer Staples: Frozen Bell Pepper Strips. Both fine for blended/cooked applications.
Scotch bonnet / habanero Fridge 1–2 weeks → Spices: Cayenne Powder (easiest sub, pantry-stable) or gochugaru.
Lemon / lime Fridge 3–4 weeks → Canned & Jarred: Bottled Lemon/Lime Juice for cooked dishes. Buy fresh when used as a raw finish (e.g. larb, piccata) — the difference is noticeable there.
Fresh cilantro Fridge (stems in water, bag over top) ~1 week No good shelf-stable sub when used raw — dried cilantro is nearly tasteless. Buy fresh only when planning a dish that week.
Fresh Thai basil Fridge (stems in water) 3–5 days Hard to substitute in pad kra pao. Regular Italian basil is a passable sub. Buy fresh for that dish specifically.
Fresh mint Fridge (stems in water) ~1 week Used raw in larb and as a serving herb. No shelf-stable sub worth using. Buy fresh as needed.
Lemongrass Fridge 2–3 weeks → Freezer Staples: Lemongrass — freeze immediately, lasts months, works just as well for simmering.
Galangal Fridge 2–3 weeks → Freezer Staples: Galangal — freeze immediately. Ginger is a sub but the flavor is noticeably different.
Kaffir lime leaves Fridge 1–2 weeks → Freezer Staples: Kaffir Lime Leaves — freeze immediately, use straight from frozen. Hard to substitute.

Canned & Jarred
🟢 All shelf-stable until opened.
Ingredient After Opening Fridge Life Notes Used In
Diced / crushed tomatoes (796ml can) Fridge 3–5 days Sub for ~3 fresh tomatoes in cooked dishes. Minimal flavor trade-off. Recommended over fresh. Recipes typically use ~400ml — freeze the rest in a zip-lock bag (squeeze out air), lasts 3–6 months. Jollof, keema, chicken tinga
Jarred roasted red peppers Fridge 1–2 weeks Drain before using. Sub for fresh bell peppers in blended/cooked applications. Slight flavor difference (sweeter, softer) — unnoticeable once cooked down. Leftovers can be frozen: drain, zip-lock, squeeze out air. Jollof base
Canned tuna (in water) Fridge (transfer to container) 2–3 days Drain well before using. Kimchi tuna pasta
Jarred minced garlic Fridge 3–4 months Sub for fresh garlic in cooked dishes. Slightly muted flavor — fine for stir-fries, stews, sauces. Not ideal for aglio e olio (use fresh there). Most recipes calling for garlic
Jarred minced ginger Fridge 3–4 months Sub for fresh ginger in cooked dishes. Convenient backup. Bulgogi, dakgalbi, keema, chicken tikka
Bottled lemon juice Fridge after opening 6 months Fine for cooked dishes and marinades. Noticeable drop in quality when used as a raw finishing squeeze — use fresh lemon/lime for that. Chicken piccata (cooked), chicken tikka marinade
Bottled lime juice Fridge after opening 6 months Same trade-off as bottled lemon juice. Larb (use fresh if possible), tom yum
Canned beans (540ml) Fridge (transfer to container) 3–4 days Drain and rinse before using. Freeze leftovers: rinse, dry, zip-lock, lasts 3–6 months. Common: black beans (chipotle bowl), chickpeas (Indian dishes; roast for snack), kidney beans (stews), cannellini (Italian soups). Chipotle beef bowl, sides, Indian and Italian dishes

Protein Source Comparison

Per 100g raw weight. A reference for choosing proteins based on your goals — lean muscle build, high protein, low unnecessary fat.

🟢 Very lean (<5g fat) 🟡 Moderate (5–15g fat) 🔴 Fatty (>15g fat)
$ = budget   $$ = moderate   $$$ = pricey
Protein Protein / 100g Fat / 100g Fat level Price Best methods Notes
Chicken breast ~31g ~3.6g 🟢 $ Pan-sear, grill, poach The leanest common protein. Core of most recipes here. Can dry out if overcooked — slice thin and cook fast.
Chicken thigh (boneless, skinless) ~26g ~9g 🟡 $ Pan-sear, grill, braise, air-fry More forgiving and juicier than breast. Slightly more fat but more flavor. Better for high-heat dishes like karaage. Often cheaper than breast.
Ground chicken ~17g ~9g 🟡 $ Stir-fry, sauté Leanest of the ground meats. Mild flavor — relies heavily on seasoning. Good for pad kra pao, larb.
Ground beef 80/20 ~17g ~20g 🔴 $ Stir-fry, long simmer Drain and rinse after cooking to cut fat significantly. Fat content is what gives it flavor — going leaner costs taste. Best for bolognese, keema, chipotle bowl.
Ground beef 90/10 ~20g ~10g 🟡 $–$$ Stir-fry, quick sauté Better macro profile than 80/20. Less flavor on its own — seasoning does more work. Dries out in long cooks; better for quick dishes.
Pork tenderloin ~22g ~3g 🟢 $$ Pan-sear, roast Leanest pork cut — comparable to chicken breast. Mild flavor. Very easy to overcook; use a thermometer or go by feel. Not commonly used in Korean cooking (shoulder/belly dominate).
Pork shoulder ~17g ~15g 🟡 $ Marinate & stir-fry, braise 돼지불고기 cut. Good balance of flavor and fat. Slice thin and marinate — fat keeps it from drying out under high heat.
Pork belly ~9g ~53g 🔴 $$ Grill, pan-fry 삼겹살. The fat IS the dish — don't try to make this lean. Eat in moderation with 김치 and lettuce wraps. Naturally self-limiting due to richness.
Salmon ~20g ~13g 🟡 $$–$$$ Pan-sear, broil, air-fry Fat here is omega-3 — beneficial, not the same as saturated fat from meat. Rich and filling. Buy frozen to save money; quality holds well. Great macro profile for the cost once you account for the omega-3 benefit.
Shrimp ~24g ~0.3g 🟢 $$ Quick sauté, stir-fry Extremely lean and fast-cooking (2–3 min). Almost pure protein. Mild flavor — relies on sauce. Buy pre-frozen; thaw in cold water 15 min. Don't overcook or they turn rubbery.
Canned tuna (in water) ~26g ~1g 🟢 $ No cooking needed Best pantry protein by value. Very high protein, almost no fat, shelf-stable. Drain well before using. Two cans = a full high-protein meal base.
Eggs ~13g ~11g 🟡 $ Fry, boil, poach, soft-boil Protein is mostly in the white; fat is in the yolk. One egg = ~6g protein. Used across many recipes as a topper — runny yolk adds richness without a heavy meal. Cheap, versatile.
Greek yogurt (0%) ~10g ~0g 🟢 $ No cooking (sauce base, marinade, topping) High protein per calorie, virtually no fat at 0%. Used as a sauce base (gochujang pasta), marinade tenderizer (chicken tikka), and topping (keema, chipotle bowl). Daily smoothie staple.

🟢 All shelf-stable dry. Store sealed.
Item Shelf Life Notes
Parboiled long-grain rice 2+ years Jollof — must say "parboiled" or "converted" on bag. Uncle Ben's Original, PC, or No Name parboiled. Do NOT use jasmine or regular long grain.
White rice (regular) 2+ years General serving rice for Korean/Thai/Indian dishes
Pasta (any shape) 2+ years Italian recipes + Korean pasta dishes
Udon noodles (dried) 1–2 years Teriyaki udon — works fine; fresh udon is better but short shelf life
Fresh udon (refrigerated) 1–2 weeks Fridge section at Korean/Asian grocery stores. Much better than dried.
